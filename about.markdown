Hi, I’m Ivan Ryabov, a software engineer in Sydney specialising in Rust and Go.

I build reliable systems and developer tools, with a particular interest in
distributed systems, testing, and architecture. Rust gives me precise control
over performance and resource use while making correctness part of the design.
Go shines when straightforward concurrency, small interfaces, and operational
simplicity matter. I care less about language camps than about choosing tools
that make software easier to reason about and maintain.

I use this site as a version-controlled, public notebook for ideas that survive
contact with real software: Rust, Go, reliability, distributed systems, testing,
architecture, and the increasingly interesting ways humans and AI build together.

The posts are personal and pragmatic. Some start with a design question; others
start with a bug, a side project, or a belief that changed after an experiment.
The goal is the same: preserve the useful reasoning, not just the final answer.

You can find my open-source work on [GitHub](https://github.com/abbyssoul/),
including [Kinjo](/projects/kinjo/), a terminal application written in Rust, or
connect with me on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/iryabov/).
