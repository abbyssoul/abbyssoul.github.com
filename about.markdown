Hi, I’m Ivan Ryabov, a software engineer in Sydney specialising in systems
software and AI-enabled engineering.

I build reliable systems and developer tools, with a particular interest in
distributed systems, testing, and architecture. My language experience spans
modern C++, Rust, and Go. I value C++ for its fine-grained control over
performance and memory. Rust brings that control together with strong
correctness guarantees, while Go shines when straightforward concurrency,
small interfaces, and operational simplicity matter. I care less about language
camps than about choosing tools that make software easier to reason about and
maintain.

My experience and interests also extend into 3D graphics, simulation,
computational physics, and real-time systems—domains where algorithms, numerical
behaviour, performance, and physical constraints meet.

I also design ways for engineers and AI agents to work together effectively:
turning repeated practices into reusable skills, giving specialist agents clear
roles and boundaries, and keeping the resulting work grounded in evidence and
human judgement.

I use this site as a version-controlled, public notebook for ideas that survive
contact with real software: C++, Rust, Go, reliability, distributed systems,
testing, architecture, 3D graphics, simulation, computational physics, real-time
systems, and the increasingly interesting ways humans and AI build together.

The posts are personal and pragmatic. Some start with a design question; others
start with a bug, a side project, or a belief that changed after an experiment.
The goal is the same: preserve the useful reasoning, not just the final answer.

You can find my open-source work on [GitHub](https://github.com/abbyssoul/),
including [Kinjo](/projects/kinjo/), a terminal application written in Rust,
and [Agent Skills](/projects/agent-skills/), my collection of reusable AI
engineering workflows. You can also connect with me on
[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/iryabov/).
